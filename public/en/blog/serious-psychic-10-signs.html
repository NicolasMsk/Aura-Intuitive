Real Psychic: 10 Signs of a Truly Gifted Medium

Laura
June 20, 2026 10 min read

In 2026, a single Google search for "online psychic" returns thousands of results. Prices that vary wildly, promises ranging from "I see everything" to "guaranteed results in 48 hours." In this saturated landscape, finding a real psychic can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack. Yet the signs that separate a genuine intuitive medium from a scammer are very real — and you don't need to be a spiritual expert to recognize them.

After 8 years of practice, I've seen many people hurt by fake mediums. Women who spent hundreds of euros on "uncrossing rituals," people who shared their deepest secrets with anonymous strangers hiding behind pseudonyms, vulnerable individuals manipulated through fear. Here's what I always advise: before trusting a psychic with your question, check these 10 signs.

Why Is It So Hard to Find a Real Psychic Today?

The democratization of online readings has been both a gift and a curse. On the positive side, anyone anywhere in the world can now access a reading — no need to travel, no appointment waiting lists, no geographic barriers. A person in rural Montana can consult a gifted practitioner in Paris just as easily as someone living in the same city.

But this openness comes with a significant downside: there is no official regulation of psychic practice in France, the UK, the United States, or virtually anywhere in the world. Anyone can call themselves a medium, a clairvoyant, or an intuitive — with no training, no certification, no accountability. The term "psychic" is legally meaningless. This creates fertile ground for predatory individuals who have learned that vulnerable, emotionally distressed people are particularly easy targets.

The people most likely to seek out a psychic are also the most likely to be hurt by a bad one. They're going through a breakup, grieving a loss, facing uncertainty about their career or their health. They need comfort, clarity, and direction. Scammers exploit this vulnerability with practiced precision — building dependency, creating fear, and draining bank accounts. This is why the 10 signs below matter so much. They aren't abstract guidelines; they're practical tools that protect real people in real moments of vulnerability.

The 10 Signs of a Genuine Psychic (Who Deserves Your Trust)

1. She Has Clear, Fixed Pricing

This is the simplest and most reliable indicator. A genuine psychic displays her prices clearly, before you've asked your first question, before you've shared any personal information, and with no ambiguity about what is included. You should be able to visit her website or profile and immediately understand: what does a reading cost, how long does it last, what does it cover?

Vague pricing — "contact me for a quote," "rates vary by case," or no pricing listed at all — is a warning sign. It gives the practitioner room to adjust her rates based on how much she thinks she can extract from you. A genuine psychic doesn't price her work based on your perceived desperation. She has a clear, consistent rate that applies to everyone equally.

At Aura Intuitive, every service has a published price visible from the homepage. There are no upsells during the reading, no "I'm seeing something serious that requires a premium consultation." The price you see is the price you pay. This is what honest practice looks like.

2. She Never Pressures You

A genuine psychic understands that a reading is an offering, not a transaction she needs to close. She presents what she perceives, answers your question, and then releases you — freely, without urgency, without guilt. There are no countdown timers on her offers. There are no "special rates available only today." There is no language designed to make you feel that if you don't act immediately, you will lose something irreplaceable.

Pressure tactics are incompatible with genuine spiritual practice. If the information she has for you is genuinely valuable, it will still be valuable tomorrow and next week. A reading isn't a flash sale. A psychic who uses urgency to drive purchases is using sales psychology — not spiritual gift — to generate income. Recognize this distinction clearly, and you will protect yourself from a significant category of exploitation.

This also extends to how a genuine psychic handles follow-up. She doesn't send repeated messages asking if you've made a decision. She doesn't suggest that your situation is deteriorating and that you need another reading urgently. She trusts that if her work resonated, you will return when you're ready — and she respects your pace entirely.

3. She Acknowledges Her Limits

No genuine psychic perceives everything. No honest practitioner claims to be infallible. Spiritual perception is a gift that operates differently depending on the day, the energies at play, the specific question, and the clarity of the connection. Some readings flow clearly and abundantly. Others are murkier, harder to pin down. A genuine psychic will tell you when this is the case.

When I encounter a question where my perception is limited or unclear, I say so. Sometimes I ask to return to the question later or approach it from a different angle. Sometimes I tell a client that I'm not the right person for this particular question, and I'll refer them elsewhere. This is what integrity looks like. It means valuing the truth of your reading over my own ego or income.

Be wary of any practitioner who claims to perceive everything perfectly, every time, with total certainty. This is not how intuitive gifts work. A practitioner making such claims is either deluding herself or deceiving you — and in either case, the result for you is the same.

4. She Doesn't Predict the Future with Absolute Certainty

This distinction is subtle but critical. A genuine psychic can perceive tendencies, currents of energy, likely trajectories based on the situation as it stands. What she cannot do — what no one can do — is guarantee an outcome. The future is not fixed. It is influenced by choices, by chance, by the decisions of others, and by energies that shift.

When a psychic says "he will call you on Thursday" or "you will receive the job offer within two weeks, guaranteed," she is overstepping what honest perception allows. These aren't readings — they're promises. And promises about outcomes that depend on free will and cosmic complexity are either reckless or manipulative.

A genuine practitioner uses careful, accurate language: "I perceive," "I sense," "the energy around this suggests," "what I'm receiving indicates." This isn't hedging or weakness — it's precision. It reflects an honest understanding of what intuitive perception is and is not. It also protects you from making major life decisions based on false certainty.

5. She Has a Real Methodology (Cards, Intuition, Felt Sense)

Every genuine psychic has developed a personal methodology over years of practice. This might be tarot or oracle cards, pendulum work, clairsentience (feeling energy in the body), clairvoyance (visual perception), or direct channeling. Whatever the method, a genuine practitioner can clearly articulate how she works and why she uses it.

At Aura Intuitive, I work primarily through intuitive perception and felt sense — a combination of what I receive visually and what I experience as physical and emotional sensations in relation to the question. I've refined this over 8 years. When clients ask how I work, I can explain it clearly, honestly, and without mystification. The method isn't secret. It's simply part of who I am as a practitioner.

A psychic who is evasive about her method, who claims to work through some proprietary technique she can't describe, or who relies entirely on vague "channeling" language with no practical grounding should be approached with caution. Genuine gifts don't require mystery to seem impressive.

6. She Never Uses Fear to Sell

This is perhaps the clearest dividing line between a genuine practitioner and a predatory one. A real psychic does not tell you that you are cursed, hexed, under a negative entity's influence, or surrounded by dark energy — and then offer to fix it for a fee. This pattern is one of the most common and damaging forms of psychic fraud.

The mechanism is psychological and powerful: you arrive vulnerable, seeking guidance. The practitioner plants a fear — something invisible, threatening, and hard to disprove. She positions herself as the only person capable of removing this threat. The fear escalates across sessions, and with it, the fees. People spend thousands in these spirals before they realize what has happened.

A genuine psychic's role is to illuminate, support, and empower — not to frighten. If something concerning arises during a reading, a genuine practitioner addresses it with care, context, and perspective. She never weaponizes it to generate dependency or income. Fear-based selling is manipulation, not spirituality. Leave immediately and without guilt if you encounter it.

7. Her Legal Disclosures Are Visible

In France, psychic services operate in a specific legal and regulatory context. Practitioners are legally required to mention that their services are for entertainment or personal development purposes only, and that readings do not constitute a substitute for professional advice (medical, legal, financial, psychological). These disclosures — often called "mentions légales" — should be accessible on any legitimate practitioner's website.

The presence of these disclosures signals that the practitioner understands and respects the boundaries of her practice. It shows she is operating transparently within the legal framework that governs this activity. It also signals that she is not making medical, legal, or financial promises — which would be both illegal and deeply irresponsible.

If you cannot find any legal disclosures on a psychic's website or social media profile, this is a significant red flag. It may indicate that the practitioner is operating informally, without accountability, or with deliberate opacity about her identity and business practices. Always verify this before sharing personal information or making any payment.

8. She Has Verifiable, Recent Reviews

Genuine practitioners build a reputation over time through the quality of their work. This reputation leaves traces: reviews on Google, Etsy, Trustpilot, or other platforms where third-party verification is possible. These reviews should be recent, varied in style (not formulaic or copy-pasted), and specific enough to reflect genuine experiences.

Look for reviews that mention specific readings, note something unexpected that resonated, or describe the experience in concrete terms. Generic praise — "amazing, highly recommend!" — can be fabricated. Reviews that describe a specific insight, a particular accuracy, or a moment of genuine connection are harder to fake and more valuable as evidence.

Also check the review platforms themselves. A practitioner with 200 reviews on her own website but none on third-party platforms should prompt questions. Self-hosted testimonials are easily manufactured. Third-party platforms have verification mechanisms that make manipulation significantly harder. For maximum confidence, seek practitioners with reviews across multiple independent platforms.

9. She Protects Your Privacy

A genuine psychic treats everything you share with her as confidential. She does not use your personal information to create dependency or leverage. She does not reference details from previous readings in ways that feel manipulative. She does not share your story — even anonymously — without your consent. Your question, your situation, and your emotional state are shared in trust, and that trust must be honored completely.

This also applies to digital practices. A genuine practitioner does not request unnecessary personal information before a reading. She does not ask for your address, your full name and date of birth, or your financial details unless they are directly relevant to the service you're purchasing. She stores any information she collects securely and transparently, in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

If a psychic seems unusually interested in gathering detailed personal information before a reading, be cautious. Cold reading — the practice of using gathered personal information to create the appearance of psychic perception — is a well-documented technique used by fraudulent practitioners. Genuine perception doesn't require a deep file on you. It requires genuine gift and honest attention.

10. She Leaves You Free to Decide

The most important sign of all. A genuine psychic's goal is your empowerment, not your dependency. A reading should leave you feeling clearer, more grounded, and better equipped to navigate your situation — not more confused, more anxious, or more convinced that you need another reading immediately to resolve what the first one opened up.

After a session with a genuine practitioner, you should feel like the protagonist of your own story. You've received information, perspective, and perhaps confirmation of what you already sensed. Now you decide what to do with it. The psychic has played her role. The choice — and the power — returns to you completely.

A practitioner who leaves you feeling that only she can see your path, that you need her continued guidance to move forward safely, that something dangerous might happen if you don't return — is not empowering you. She is creating dependency. This is the opposite of genuine spiritual service, regardless of how gifted she might actually be.

5 Scam Red Flags to Run From

1. The "Unlimited Free Reading" That Becomes Paid

This is one of the most common entry points for psychic fraud, and it's effective precisely because it feels generous at first. The offer is simple: a free reading, no strings attached, often framed as a "special promotion" or a "gift" to attract new clients. You accept. The reading begins.

What follows is a carefully scripted escalation. The initial reading ends at a crucial moment — just as something "significant" is being revealed, or just as a "pressing situation" is identified. To find out more, to receive the complete reading, to learn how to address the issue that's just been uncovered, you need to pay. The "free" reading was never meant to be complete. It was a hook, not a gift.

The most sophisticated versions of this scam continue for months. Each paid session ends at another crucial juncture. The amount requested escalates gradually. By the time the pattern is clear, the person has often spent far more than they ever intended. The warning sign is simple: nothing of genuine value is truly unlimited and free. If it sounds too generous to be real, trust that instinct.

2. The €500 Uncrossing Ritual

"I'm sensing a negative energy block around you — possibly a hex placed by someone close to you. I can remove it, but the ritual requires specific materials and several hours of my time. The cost is €500." This script, or variations of it, is used by fraudulent practitioners around the world. It works because it combines fear with a solution: the problem is invisible and threatening, and only this specific person can fix it.

The price point is chosen carefully — high enough to seem significant and therefore credible (a cheap ritual couldn't address something this serious), but not so high as to immediately trigger disbelief. Once you've paid, the problem is "partially resolved" but requires follow-up treatments. The cycle repeats. Some people have reported spending tens of thousands of euros in these spirals before breaking free.

The fundamental truth is this: no genuine spiritual practitioner can remove a "curse" placed on you by someone else for a flat fee. Spiritual protection and energetic clearing are practices that you participate in actively — they are not services that can be purchased like a product. Any practitioner selling "removals" for fixed prices is exploiting your fear, not serving your spiritual wellbeing.

3. Fear-Based Emotional Manipulation ("You're Cursed")

Beyond the uncrossing ritual scam, fear is used more broadly as a control mechanism. A fraudulent practitioner might tell you that a loved one is in spiritual danger, that your relationship is doomed unless immediate action is taken, that you are surrounded by negative entities that are affecting your health and fortune. These statements are designed to destabilize, not to inform.

The emotional impact is deliberately disorienting. When you're frightened, your critical thinking weakens. You're more likely to act impulsively, to spend money you wouldn't otherwise spend, to return for follow-up sessions you don't need. Fear is the single most powerful tool in the fraudulent psychic's arsenal, and recognizing it for what it is — manipulation, not perception — is one of the most important forms of self-protection available to you.

A genuine reading may sometimes surface difficult information. A genuine practitioner might perceive tension, obstacles, or challenges ahead. The difference is in how this is communicated: with care, with context, with the goal of helping you navigate rather than frightening you into compliance. Any information designed primarily to frighten rather than illuminate is a weapon, not a gift.

4. Premium-Rate Phone Lines

Premium-rate phone lines — where each minute of the call costs several euros — are a business model built around extending call duration rather than serving the caller. The incentive structure is fundamentally opposed to genuine psychic practice. The longer the call, the more revenue generated; therefore, the incentive is to delay, repeat, add tangents, and create reasons to stay on the line rather than deliver concise, accurate information.

This doesn't mean everyone working on such platforms is dishonest — but the business model creates perverse incentives that undermine genuine service. The most egregious practitioners in this space have been known to keep vulnerable callers on the line for hours, running up bills of hundreds of euros in a single call. Some have built entire "reader" personas with scripted responses designed to maximize call duration.

If you choose to use a phone-based reading service, set a hard time limit before you call and stick to it. Better still, seek practitioners who work through written or email-based readings where the incentive to extend the session artificially is removed. Fixed-price services with a clear scope are always safer than per-minute billing.

5. Anonymous Psychics With No Verifiable Identity

A practitioner who hides behind a pseudonym, uses only a first name, has no verifiable social media presence, and cannot be traced to any real person is a practitioner who has made a deliberate choice to avoid accountability. This is not spirituality; it is strategic invisibility designed to enable exploitation without consequences.

Genuine practitioners stand behind their work publicly. They use their real name or a clearly identified professional name that is linked to a traceable identity. They have TikTok or YouTube content, client reviews on third-party platforms, or other digital footprints that confirm they are a real person with a genuine practice history. You should be able to verify, independently and without the practitioner's assistance, that she is who she says she is.

Before any reading, spend five minutes searching the practitioner's name or business name. Look for their website, their social profiles, their reviews. If nothing comes up — or worse, if you find reports from other people who felt scammed — trust what you find. Anonymity is not modesty. In the context of psychic services, it is almost always a warning sign.

KEY TAKEAWAY

A genuine psychic has nothing to hide. Her identity, pricing, method and legal disclosures are all accessible before you ask your first question. If any of this information is vague or absent, treat it as a warning sign.

How to Verify a Psychic's Authenticity Before Your Reading

Before committing to any reading — paid or free — invest five to ten minutes in verification. It's time well spent and can save you significant distress and expense. Here are the practical steps I recommend to anyone considering an online psychic.

Search the practitioner's name plus "reviews" on Google. This simple search will often surface both positive testimonials and, if there are problems, reports of dissatisfying or fraudulent experiences. Don't stop at the first result — scroll through several pages, especially if the name is common.

Check Trustpilot, Etsy, and Google Business reviews independently, not through links provided by the practitioner herself. Third-party reviews are much harder to manipulate than testimonials on a personal website. Look for volume, recency, and specificity. A practitioner with 50 detailed reviews from the past six months is far more credible than one with 500 generic testimonials accumulated over years.

Verify that legal disclosures are visible on her site. In France, these are required by law. If they are absent, it may indicate that the practitioner is operating without awareness of or respect for the legal framework — or is deliberately avoiding the accountability that comes with transparent disclosure.

Check her prices before any commitment or information-sharing. If pricing is not clearly stated before you've provided your name, email, or any personal information, treat this as a red flag. Genuine practitioners display their prices openly. Vague pricing structures exist to allow exploitation, not to serve you.

Watch her TikTok or YouTube content before booking. Social media content is one of the best ways to assess a psychic's personality, values, and authenticity. Does she communicate with care and integrity? Does she use fear or urgency in her content? Does she acknowledge her limits, or does she project absolute certainty? Content reveals character. Spend 15 minutes watching before you spend a single euro.

Frequently Asked Questions

How to recognize a genuine psychic medium?

A genuine psychic displays clear, fixed pricing, never uses fear tactics to push you to spend more, acknowledges her limits without claiming to know everything, has verifiable and recent reviews, and her legal disclosures are visible on her site. She lets you leave freely, with no pressure or forced commitment.

How to avoid psychic scams?

Avoid psychics who offer "unlimited free consultations" that become paid, those who ask for your payment details quickly, those who create fear ("you are cursed") to sell you rituals for €500, premium-rate phone lines, and practitioners with no verifiable identity online.

Can a real psychic ever be wrong?

Yes. A genuine psychic openly acknowledges her limitations and never claims to perceive with absolute certainty. Spiritual perception is a form of intuition, not mathematical certainty. An honest practitioner says "I perceive" rather than "I know for certain." The dishonesty lies precisely in claiming to never be wrong.

What is the difference between a psychic and a medium?

A psychic perceives information about your past, present, and future through clairvoyant gifts. A medium establishes contact with spiritual entities — deceased loved ones, guides, angels. In practice, many practitioners have both gifts. Laura, for example, is both an intuitive psychic and a medium.

How much does a genuine psychic reading cost?

Prices vary depending on the practitioner and service. At Aura Intuitive, readings start at €1 for a Yes/No Answer and €10 for a full Intuitive Reading. A genuine psychic always displays her prices clearly, with no hidden fees or pressure to add services.
