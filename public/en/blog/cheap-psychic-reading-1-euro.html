€1 Psychic Reading: Legit or a Scam?

March 7, 2026 Reading time: 6 min

You've seen a €1 psychic reading advertised and your first instinct was to write it off as a scam. That's completely understandable. Online, offers that seem too good to be true usually are. But sometimes a low price has a simple explanation. And this is one of those times.

Why €1 and not €30?

Most online psychics work through platforms that take a large commission — sometimes 50 to 70% of the consultation fee. Add per-minute billing, phone operator charges, advertising costs… and you understand why a reading can easily run to €30, €50 or even €80.

At Aura Intuitive, there are no middlemen. No platform, no commission, no call centre. I work alone, from home. Payment goes directly through Stripe, a secure payment service. The result: I can offer a short format at €1 without cutting corners on quality.

What do you receive for €1?

The €1 format is the Yes / No Answer. You ask one closed question — a question that can be answered yes or no — and you receive my answer by email within 24 hours.

This is not an automatically generated yes or no. I connect to your energy, take the time to feel into your situation, and share what I perceive. The answer always includes a short personalised message alongside the yes or no.

Examples of suitable questions

"Is he still thinking about me?" — "Should I accept this job offer?" — "Is this person being genuine with me?" — "Is my instinct about this situation right?"

What are the limits of a €1 reading?

To be honest: for €1, you don't receive a full intuitive reading spanning 15 paragraphs. The Yes/No format is designed for simple, direct questions. If your situation is complex, if you need to understand the deeper dynamics or explore several aspects, the Intuitive Reading at €10 will serve you better.

The Yes/No format also doesn't allow for multiple questions. It's one question, one answer. If you have several, you can book as many sessions as you like — each at €1.

Red flags vs. a real service

When you come across a low-cost psychic offer, here's how to tell the difference between a genuine service and a scam:

  • Scam: "10 free minutes" followed by automatic per-minute billing. Legitimate: fixed price stated upfront, no surprises.
  • Scam: they call you back, create urgency, try to frighten you. Legitimate: no follow-ups, no pressure whatsoever.
  • Scam: hidden subscription buried in the terms and conditions. Legitimate: one-time payment, no recurring charges.
  • Scam: a generic answer that could apply to anyone. Legitimate: a personalised answer based on your specific question and situation.

Why start with the Yes/No

Many people consulting for the first time are hesitant to invest in a full reading. That's completely understandable. The Yes/No at €1 lets you test the service without any financial risk.
